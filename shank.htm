Shank

Shanks are usually made of leather, plastic, cardstock, steel or layers of glue-hardened burlap. The flexibility of shanks is determined by the shanks thickness and the type of material used. The thickness may be consistent throughout the shoe or it may vary along its length to produce different strengths at select points. For example, slits may be cut across a shank at demi-pointe to enhance the roll through. Also, a shank's thickness may transition at some point along its length in order to implement differing strengths above and below the transition. Standard pointe shoes typically have a full shank, where the shank runs the full length of the sole, or fractional length shanks. Many pointe shoe manufacturers will offer a choice of shank materials, and some will build shoes with customized shanks of different stiffness and lengths. Back
